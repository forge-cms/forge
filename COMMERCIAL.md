# Commercial use

Forge is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL).

## What this means for you

**Individual developers, open source projects, and companies building their
own sites with Forge:** AGPL imposes no meaningful restriction. You can use,
modify, and deploy Forge freely.

**Organisations providing a hosted service to others using Forge:** AGPL
requires you to release your modifications under the same license. If this
is a constraint, a commercial license is available — see below.

## Future commercial license

When a hosted offering ("Forge Cloud") becomes available, a commercial
license will be available for organisations that want to use Forge as the
basis of a hosted service without the AGPL obligation.

This will never affect the open source core. The AGPL-licensed framework
you use today will remain AGPL forever.

## Contributor License Agreement

Contributors are asked to sign a simple CLA before their first pull request
is merged. This grants forge-cms the right to include your contributions in
commercial offerings and to issue commercial license exemptions.

The CLA is handled automatically via cla-assistant.io when you open a PR.

## Questions

If you have questions about commercial use, licensing, or the CLA,
open an issue tagged `licensing`.
